1. Create a Calorie Deficit

Consume fewer calories than your body burns.

  • Start with a 10–20% reduction based on your maintenance level.
  • Use apps or calculators to estimate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

2. Prioritize Protein Intake

Retain muscle while losing fat.

  • Aim for 1.0–1.2 grams of protein per pound of body weight daily.
  • Sources: Chicken breast, fish, eggs, low-fat dairy, tofu, and legumes.

3. Choose Complex Carbohydrates

Provide sustained energy and curb cravings.

  • Opt for whole grains (brown rice, oats), sweet potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Adjust carb intake based on activity levels.

4. Incorporate Healthy Fats

Supports hormones and overall health.

  • Target 20–30% of daily calories from fats.
  • Sources: Avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish like salmon.

5. Focus on Nutrient-Dense Foods

Maximize satiety and prevent nutrient deficiencies.

  • Eat plenty of vegetables (broccoli, spinach, bell peppers) and fruits (berries, apples, oranges).
  • Avoid empty-calorie foods like sugary snacks and fried items.

6. Stay Hydrated

Proper hydration helps control hunger and improves workout performance.

  • Drink at least 8–10 glasses of water daily.

7. Manage Meal Timing

Space meals evenly to stabilize energy levels.

  • Pre- and post-workout: Carbs for energy, protein for recovery.

8. Monitor Portion Sizes

Practice mindful eating:

  • Use smaller plates and a food scale for accuracy.
  • Eat slowly and avoid distractions.

9. Avoid Liquid Calories

Eliminate sugary drinks, juices, and alcohol.

  • Choose black coffee, green tea, or zero-calorie beverages.

10. Adjust Gradually

Reassess progress every 2–3 weeks.

  • Adjust calorie intake or activity levels as needed to continue fat loss.
