1. Calculate Your Maintenance Calories

Consume the same calories as your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE).

  • Use online calculators or apps to determine your TDEE.
  • Track your intake for accuracy.

2. Balance Macronutrients

Maintain energy levels and body composition.

  • Protein: 0.8–1.2 grams per pound of body weight.
  • Carbs: 45–55% of daily calories.
  • Fats: 25–35% of daily calories.

3. Focus on Nutrient-Dense Foods

Ensure essential vitamins and minerals.

  • Include vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean protein, and healthy fats.
  • Limit processed foods and added sugars.

4. Stay Consistent with Meal Timing

Spread meals evenly to maintain stable energy levels.

  • Include protein, carbs, and fats in every meal.

5. Stay Hydrated

Drink water regularly for digestion and metabolism.

  • Aim for 8–10 glasses daily, or more if active.

6. Control Portions Mindfully

Avoid overeating by practicing portion control.

  • Use smaller plates and measure servings if necessary.
  • Listen to hunger and fullness cues.

7. Manage Stress and Sleep

Prevent weight fluctuations.

  • Aim for 7–8 hours of quality sleep nightly.
  • Practice stress relief like meditation or exercise.

8. Include Physical Activity

Maintain weight and health.

  • Combine cardio, strength training, and flexibility exercises.

9. Limit Liquid Calories

Avoid unnecessary calories from sugary drinks and alcohol.

  • Choose water, herbal teas, or low-calorie drinks.

10. Monitor Progress and Adjust

Stay consistent with weight and measurements.

  • Adjust intake or activity levels if changes occur.
